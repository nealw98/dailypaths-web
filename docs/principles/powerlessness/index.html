Powerlessness & Surrender

Accepting what we cannot control and letting go of the rest.

“Surrender isn’t giving up — it’s giving over.”

Surrendering the Illusion of Control

Step 1 is often the hardest pill to swallow because it requires us to admit that our best thinking hasn’t worked. We admitted we were powerless over alcohol—and that our lives had become unmanageable. To the newcomer, “powerless” sounds like weakness. But in 30 years of recovery, I’ve seen that Step 1 is actually the ultimate act of courage.

Admitting unmanageability means we stop trying to do the impossible. We stop trying to “manage” the unmanageable disease in another person. The “powerlessness” isn’t about the alcoholic; it’s about our own futile attempts to control them. When we stop fighting the reality of the situation, we stop losing the battle.

The magic of Step 1, however, is found in the very first word: “We.” Recovery doesn’t happen in isolation. It happens when we peer through the window and realize that there are others who have felt exactly what we feel. By admitting we can’t do it alone, we open the door to a community that can walk the path with us. Powerlessness over the disease is where our power over our own lives finally begins.
