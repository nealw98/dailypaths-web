Step Three

FAITH & THE FREEDOM OF LETTING GO

Made a decision to turn our will and our lives over to the care of God as we understood Him.

This Step means making a conscious effort to stop relying entirely on self-will. It means we stop trying to force our will on others. Instead, it asks us to place our concerns, fears, and daily lives into the care of a Higher Power.

For the Al-Anon member, taking this Step is the ultimate practice of the slogan “Let Go and Let God.” It brings enormous relief by lifting the heavy burden of responsibility for the alcoholic—and for the universe—off our shoulders. It means moving from the exhausting attitude of control and self-sufficiency and into a trusting reliance on a power greater than ourselves. In Al-Anon, we learn to focus on the next right action and trust the outcomes to our Higher Power.

The core spiritual principles of Step Three are surrender and trust. We need to be willing to let go of fear and replace it with trust. It requires the belief that we will be cared for, even when we let go of the reins. This Step is vitally important because it allows us to focus on today's actions and let go of our anxiety for the future. We begin to actively seek spiritual guidance. It means trusting the Al-Anon program and continuing to attend meetings and work the remaining steps with our sponsor.

Questions for Reflection

Take your time with these questions. There are no right answers — only honest ones.

  • What does “letting go” mean to me in practical terms?
  • In what areas of my life am I still trying to be in charge of outcomes?
  • How might my life change if I trusted a Higher Power with my worries?
  • What is the difference between making a decision and taking an action?
  • Can I turn over my will without knowing what will happen next?
