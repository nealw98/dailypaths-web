The Twelve Steps

The Twelve Steps of Al-Anon offer a path to personal recovery for anyone affected by someone else’s drinking. Each step builds on the one before it, guiding us from powerlessness toward a spiritual awakening.

In Al-Anon Daily Paths, each month’s readings focus on one step — Step 1 in January through Step 12 in December. Select a step below to explore it with questions for reflection.

1

Step 1

We admitted we were powerless over alcohol — that our lives had become unmanageable.

January · Honesty
2

Step 2

Came to believe that a Power greater than ourselves could restore us to sanity.

February · Hope
3

Step 3

Made a decision to turn our will and our lives over to the care of God as we understood Him.

March · Faith
4

Step 4

Made a searching and fearless moral inventory of ourselves.

April · Courage
5

Step 5

Admitted to God, to ourselves, and to another human being the exact nature of our wrongs.

May · Integrity
6

Step 6

Were entirely ready to have God remove all these defects of character.

June · Willingness
7

Step 7

Humbly asked Him to remove our shortcomings.

July · Humility
8

Step 8

Made a list of all persons we had harmed, and became willing to make amends to them all.

August · Brotherly Love
9

Step 9

Made direct amends to such people wherever possible, except when to do so would injure them or others.

September · Justice
10

Step 10

Continued to take personal inventory and when we were wrong promptly admitted it.

October · Perseverance
11

Step 11

Sought through prayer and meditation to improve our conscious contact with God as we understood Him, praying only for knowledge of His will for us and the power to carry that out.

November · Spiritual Awareness
12

Step 12

Having had a spiritual awakening as the result of these steps, we tried to carry this message to others, and to practice these principles in all our affairs.

December · Service

The Twelve Steps are adapted from Alcoholics Anonymous and used by Al-Anon Family Groups. For the official Al-Anon perspective on the Steps, visit al-anon.org.
